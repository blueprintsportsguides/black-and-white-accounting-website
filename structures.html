Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about business structures.

How do I know which business structure is right for me?

+

It depends on your income, risk profile, growth plans, and personal circumstances. We'll talk you through the options, explain the tax and legal implications of each, and help you choose the structure that best fits where you are now and where you want to be.

Can I change my business structure later?

+

Yes, and many businesses do as they grow. Moving from sole trader to limited company is one of the most common changes. We can advise on timing, tax implications, and handle the transition for you so it's as smooth as possible.

What's the difference between a sole trader and a limited company?

+

A sole trader is the simplest structure - you and the business are one legal entity. A limited company is a separate legal entity, which can offer tax advantages and limited liability, but comes with more reporting and compliance requirements. We can help you weigh up the pros and cons for your situation.

Do I need to set up a limited company to save tax?

+

Not always. While a limited company can offer tax efficiencies at certain income levels, it's not right for everyone. We'll look at your full picture - income, expenses, plans - and give you an honest recommendation rather than a one-size-fits-all answer.

What are the ongoing obligations for each structure?

+

Each structure has different filing, reporting, and tax obligations. Sole traders file Self Assessment annually. Limited companies have Corporation Tax returns, annual accounts, and Companies House filings. We handle all of this for you, so you stay compliant without the stress.
