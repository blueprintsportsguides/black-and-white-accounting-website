If you're self-employed or a landlord, MTD for Income Tax Self Assessment (MTD ITSA) means digital records and quarterly updates instead of one annual return. We'll help you get set up and stay compliant — without the stress.

Will MTD affect you?

MTD ITSA rolls out in stages based on your total gross income from self-employment and property (combined).

From April 2026

Mandatory if your combined income is over £50,000.

From April 2027

Threshold reduces to over £30,000.

From April 2028

Threshold reduces to over £20,000.

What actually changes with MTD?

The biggest shift is moving from annual admin to a simple, repeatable quarterly routine — supported by HMRC-approved software.

Digital records

Keep your income and expenses digitally throughout the year using MTD-compatible software.

Quarterly updates

Send quarterly updates to HMRC (plus an end-of-year final declaration).

Software & bridging

You’ll need a tool that can connect to HMRC. We’ll recommend the right setup and implement it.

Less panic, more visibility

You’ll get a clearer view of your numbers during the year, not just at the deadline.

MTD FAQs (quick answers)

Do I need to do quarterly tax payments?

MTD requires quarterly updates to HMRC. It doesn’t automatically mean quarterly payments, but it does change your reporting rhythm.

What if I use spreadsheets?

You can still keep records in spreadsheets, but you’ll need an MTD-compatible method (often software or bridging) to submit updates. We’ll advise the simplest option.

What’s the fastest way to get ready?

Start early: choose software, set up categories, and build a quarterly routine. We can implement the setup and train you so you’re confident well before the deadline.
