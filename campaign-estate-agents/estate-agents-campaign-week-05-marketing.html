Incorporation Relief for Landlords: Change from April 2026

Some landlords transfer their property portfolio into a limited company for tax or commercial reasons. Until now, Incorporation Relief could apply automatically when certain conditions were met, deferring Capital Gains Tax on the transfer. From 6 April 2026, that relief will no longer apply by default. It must be claimed, and there are strict time limits. Missing the claim deadline can mean an immediate CGT bill.

If any of your clients are considering moving their rental properties into a company, they should take advice well before April 2026. We can advise on structure, reliefs and timing so they do not get caught out.

Why this matters

  • Incorporation Relief must be actively claimed from April 2026
  • Deadlines are tight; missing them can trigger full CGT
  • Planning now avoids last-minute decisions and errors
  • We advise on structure and reliefs for property transfers
