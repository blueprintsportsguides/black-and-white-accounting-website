Capital Gains Tax on Property: What Your Clients Need to Know

HMRC's latest figures show Capital Gains Tax receipts from property have risen sharply. Many landlords are selling or restructuring, and CGT can take a big bite out of the proceeds if it is not planned for. From April 2025, rates on residential property gains are 18% for basic rate taxpayers and 24% for higher rate taxpayers. The annual exemption is £3,000.

When a landlord sells a rental property, reliefs such as Principal Private Residence relief (if the property was once their main home) and lettings relief can make a real difference. Getting the timing and reporting right also matters: gains must be reported and paid within 60 days of completion for UK residential property.
