Sole Trade Accounts Services

Financial statements prepared for self-employed individuals operating as sole traders. Required for HMRC Self Assessment tax returns, with no Companies House filing needed.

What are Sole Trade Accounts?

Sole trade accounts are financial statements prepared for self-employed individuals operating as sole traders. Unlike limited companies, sole traders don't need to file accounts with Companies House, but they must maintain proper records and prepare accounts for HMRC Self Assessment tax returns.

Who Needs Sole Trade Accounts?

Sole trade accounts are required for self-employed individuals operating as sole traders.

Self-Employed Individuals

Trading under their own name

Freelancers & Consultants

Operating as sole traders

Contractors

Working independently

Trading Income

Anyone with income above £1,000 trading allowance (2025/26)

Legal Requirements (2026)

Important deadlines and requirements for sole traders:

  • Record Keeping Keep business records for at least 5 years after the 31 January filing deadline
  • Self Assessment Submit tax return by 31 January following the tax year end
  • Tax Payment Pay tax and Class 4 National Insurance by 31 January (or in instalments)
  • VAT Registration Register for VAT if turnover exceeds £90,000 (2026 threshold)

What's Included

Our sole trade accounts service includes:

  • Trading Profit & Loss Account Income and expenses for the tax year
  • Balance Sheet Assets (equipment, vehicles, stock) and liabilities
  • Capital Account Owner's capital introduced and drawings
  • Expense Analysis Detailed breakdown of allowable business expenses
  • Tax Computation Calculation of taxable profit for Self Assessment
  • Supporting Schedules Breakdown of income sources and expense categories
