Management Accounts

Internal financial reports prepared regularly to help business owners make informed decisions. Not filed with Companies House but essential for managing and growing your business.

What are Management Accounts?

Management accounts are internal financial reports prepared regularly (typically monthly or quarterly) to help business owners and directors make informed decisions. Unlike statutory accounts, they are not filed with Companies House but are essential tools for managing and growing your business.

Who Needs Management Accounts?

Management accounts are valuable for businesses that need regular financial insights to make strategic decisions.

Growing Businesses

Need regular financial insights to make strategic decisions and track progress.

Limited Companies

Directors benefit from monthly financial performance reviews.

Businesses Seeking Finance

Banks and investors often require regular management accounts.

Seasonal Businesses

Help identify trends and plan for busy and quiet periods.

What We Provide

Our management accounts package includes everything you need to understand your business performance:

  • Profit & Loss Statement Monthly or quarterly income and expenses
  • Balance Sheet Assets, liabilities, and equity position
  • Cash Flow Statement Cash inflows and outflows, highlighting liquidity
  • Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) Gross margin, net profit, debtors, creditors
  • Budget vs Actual Analysis Compare performance against forecasts
  • Trend Analysis Identify patterns and seasonal variations
  • Commentary and Insights Explanation of numbers and recommendations

How Often Do You Need Them?

We typically prepare management accounts on a schedule that works for your business:

Monthly

For active, growing businesses needing regular oversight

Quarterly

For established businesses with stable operations

Ad-hoc

For specific decision-making periods or special projects

Benefits of Management Accounts

Regular management accounts give you the insights you need to make better business decisions.

Informed Decisions

Make decisions based on real-time financial data rather than guesswork.

Early Problem Detection

Identify problems before they become critical and costly to fix.

Cash Flow Management

Improve cash flow planning and management with regular visibility.

Finance Support

Support loan applications and investor discussions with professional reports.

Goal Tracking

Track progress towards business goals and adjust strategies accordingly.

Benchmarking

Benchmark performance against industry standards and best practices.
