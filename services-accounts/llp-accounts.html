LLP Accounts

Statutory financial statements for Limited Liability Partnerships, combining partnership flexibility with limited liability protection. Similar filing requirements to limited companies.

What are LLP Accounts?

LLP (Limited Liability Partnership) accounts are statutory financial statements that all UK LLPs must prepare and file annually with Companies House. LLPs combine the flexibility of a partnership with the limited liability protection of a company, and have similar filing requirements to limited companies, including the need to file accounts with Companies House.

Who Must File LLP Accounts?

All Limited Liability Partnerships registered in the UK must prepare and file accounts.

Trading LLPs

All active trading LLPs must file full accounts

Professional LLPs

Solicitors, accountants, architects, and other professional services

Dormant LLPs

Dormant LLPs must file simplified dormant accounts

All LLP Sizes

Micro-entity, small, medium, and large LLPs all have filing obligations

Filing Requirements (2026)

Important deadlines and requirements for filing LLP accounts:

1

Filing Deadline

9 months after the end of the accounting period

2

Late Filing Penalties

£150 to £1,500 depending on how late and LLP size

3

Tax Returns

LLP tax return (SA800) and individual member Self Assessment returns required

4

Electronic Filing

All accounts must be filed electronically with Companies House

What's Included

LLP accounts typically include the following components:

  • Profit & Loss Account Income, expenses, and profit/loss for the accounting period
  • Balance Sheet LLP assets, liabilities, and members' capital accounts
  • Members' Interests Shows each member's capital account and share of profit
  • Cash Flow Statement Cash movements during the period (required for medium/large LLPs)
  • Notes to the Accounts Detailed explanations, accounting policies, and breakdowns
  • Designated Members' Report Overview of business activities and performance
