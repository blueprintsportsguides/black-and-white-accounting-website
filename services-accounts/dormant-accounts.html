Dormant Accounts

Simplified statutory accounts for companies with no significant accounting transactions during the financial year. Still required to file with Companies House.

What are Dormant Accounts?

Dormant accounts are simplified statutory accounts prepared for companies that have had no significant accounting transactions during the financial year. A company is considered dormant if it has had no 'significant accounting transactions' - meaning no transactions that are required to be entered in the company's accounting records (excluding fees paid to Companies House, penalties, and shares issued for cash).

When is a Company Dormant?

A company is considered dormant if it has had no significant accounting transactions during the period.

No Trading Activity

Company has not traded or received income during the period

No Significant Transactions

Only permitted transactions (fees, penalties, share issues for cash)

Holding Companies

Some holding companies may qualify if only holding investments

Future Use

Companies set up but not yet trading

Filing Requirements (2026)

Important deadlines and requirements for dormant accounts:

  • Filing Deadline 9 months after the end of the accounting period (same as trading companies)
  • Simplified Accounts Dormant accounts are much simpler than full accounts
  • Late Filing Penalties Same penalties apply (£150 to £1,500) if filed late
  • Confirmation Statement Still required to file confirmation statement annually

What's Included

Dormant accounts are much simpler than full accounts and typically include:

✓ Balance Sheet

Simplified balance sheet showing assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity

✗ No Profit & Loss

No profit & loss account required (as there are no transactions)

✓ Notes to Accounts

Minimal notes confirming the company was dormant

✓ Director's Statement

Statement confirming the company was dormant throughout the period

✗ No Director's Report

Director's report not required for dormant companies
