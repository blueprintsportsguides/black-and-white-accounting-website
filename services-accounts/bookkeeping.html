Bookkeeping Services

Systematic recording of all financial transactions in your business. Essential for accurate tax returns and financial statements, and a legal requirement for all UK businesses.

What is Bookkeeping?

Bookkeeping is the systematic recording of all financial transactions in your business. It involves maintaining accurate records of income, expenses, assets, and liabilities on a day-to-day basis. In the UK, proper bookkeeping is a legal requirement for all businesses and is essential for preparing accurate tax returns and financial statements.

Who Needs Bookkeeping?

All businesses in the UK, regardless of size or structure, need to maintain proper bookkeeping records.

Sole Traders

Required to keep records for Self Assessment tax returns

Limited Companies

Legal requirement under the Companies Act 2006

Partnerships & LLPs

Required for partnership tax returns and statutory accounts

VAT-Registered Businesses

Must maintain detailed records for VAT returns

Legal Requirements (2026)

Under UK law, businesses must meet these compliance requirements:

  • Record Keeping Keep records for at least 6 years (5 years for limited companies from the end of the accounting period)
  • Digital Records Keep digital records if using Making Tax Digital (MTD) for VAT (mandatory for VAT-registered businesses)
  • Accuracy Maintain records that are sufficient to calculate tax liability
  • Completeness Ensure records are accurate, complete, and readable
