Partnership Accounts

Financial statements prepared for businesses operating as partnerships. Required for HMRC partnership tax returns and individual partner Self Assessment returns.

What are Partnership Accounts?

Partnership accounts are financial statements prepared for businesses operating as partnerships (two or more people carrying on a business together with a view to profit). Unlike limited companies, partnerships don't file accounts with Companies House, but they must prepare accounts for HMRC partnership tax returns and to calculate each partner's share of profit for their individual Self Assessment returns.

Who Needs Partnership Accounts?

Partnership accounts are required for all businesses operating as partnerships.

General Partnerships

Two or more partners carrying on business together

Professional Partnerships

Solicitors, accountants, doctors, and other professionals

Trading Partnerships

Any business with two or more owners sharing profits

Family Partnerships

Family members running a business together

Legal Requirements (2026)

Important deadlines and requirements for partnerships:

  • Partnership Tax Return Must file partnership tax return (SA800) by 31 January following the tax year end
  • Partner Self Assessment Each partner must include their share of profit in their Self Assessment return
  • Record Keeping Keep business records for at least 5 years after the 31 January filing deadline
  • Profit Allocation Accounts must clearly show each partner's share of profit or loss

What's Included

Partnership accounts typically include the following components:

  • Trading Profit & Loss Account Income and expenses for the accounting period
  • Appropriation Account Shows how profit is allocated between partners (salaries, interest on capital, profit shares)
  • Balance Sheet Partnership assets, liabilities, and each partner's capital account
  • Partner Capital Accounts Shows each partner's capital introduced, drawings, and share of profit
  • Expense Analysis Detailed breakdown of allowable business expenses
  • Tax Computation Calculation of taxable profit for partnership and individual partner returns
