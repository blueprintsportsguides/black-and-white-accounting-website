Group Accounts

Consolidated financial statements combining parent company and subsidiary results into a single set of accounts. Required for companies with subsidiaries.

What are Group Accounts?

Group accounts (also known as consolidated accounts) are financial statements that combine the results of a parent company and its subsidiaries into a single set of accounts. They provide a comprehensive view of the entire group's financial performance and position, treating the group as a single economic entity. Group accounts are required when a company has subsidiaries and must be filed with Companies House alongside the parent company's individual accounts.

Who Must File Group Accounts?

Group accounts are required for companies with subsidiaries, with some exemptions.

Parent Companies

Companies with one or more subsidiaries must prepare group accounts

Holding Companies

Companies that hold shares in other companies

Small Groups

Small groups may be exempt if they meet certain criteria

Medium/Large Groups

Medium and large groups must always prepare group accounts

Filing Requirements (2026)

Important deadlines and requirements for group accounts:

  • Filing Deadline 9 months after the end of the accounting period (same as individual accounts)
  • Both Sets Required Must file both individual parent company accounts and consolidated group accounts
  • Small Group Exemption Small groups may be exempt if parent and group meet small company criteria
  • Complex Requirements Group accounts are more complex and require elimination of inter-company transactions

What's Included

Group accounts typically include the following components:

Consolidated Balance Sheet

Combined assets, liabilities, and equity of the entire group

Consolidated Profit & Loss

Combined income and expenses of all group companies

Consolidated Cash Flow

Cash movements across the entire group (required for medium/large groups)

Elimination Adjustments

Elimination of inter-company transactions, balances, and investments

Notes to Accounts

Detailed explanations, accounting policies, and subsidiary information

Director's Report

Overview of group activities, performance, and future developments
