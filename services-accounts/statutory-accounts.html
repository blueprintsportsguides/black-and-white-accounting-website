Annual / Statutory Accounts

Formal financial statements that limited companies must prepare and file with Companies House each year. Comprehensive overview of financial performance and position.

What are Statutory Accounts?

Statutory accounts (also known as annual accounts) are formal financial statements that limited companies must prepare and file with Companies House each year. They provide a comprehensive overview of a company's financial performance and position for the accounting period, typically covering 12 months.

Who Must File Statutory Accounts?

All limited companies registered in the UK must prepare and file statutory accounts, regardless of size or trading status.

Active Trading Companies

All trading limited companies

Dormant Companies

Simplified accounts required

All Company Sizes

Micro-entity, small, medium, and large companies

Filing Requirements (2026)

Important deadlines and requirements for filing statutory accounts:

  • Filing Deadline 9 months after the end of the accounting period
  • Late Filing Penalties £150 to £1,500 depending on how late and company size
  • Abbreviated Accounts Small companies can file simplified accounts
  • Micro-entity Accounts Micro-entities can file even simpler accounts

Company Size Thresholds (2026)

Filing requirements vary by company size:

Micro-entity

Turnover ≤ £632,000, Balance sheet ≤ £316,000, Employees ≤ 10

Small Company

Turnover ≤ £10.2m, Balance sheet ≤ £5.1m, Employees ≤ 50

Medium Company

Turnover ≤ £36m, Balance sheet ≤ £18m, Employees ≤ 250

Large Company

Exceeds medium company thresholds

What's Included

Statutory accounts typically include the following components:

  • Balance Sheet Company's assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity at the year-end
  • Profit & Loss Account Income, expenses, and profit/loss for the period
  • Cash Flow Statement Cash movements during the period (required for medium/large companies)
  • Notes to the Accounts Detailed explanations and breakdowns
  • Director's Report Overview of business activities and performance
  • Auditor's Report If audit is required (typically for large companies)
