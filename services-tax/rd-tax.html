Research & Development Tax

Government incentive for innovation. Claim enhanced tax relief on qualifying R&D expenditure, either as Corporation Tax reduction or cash credit for loss-making companies.

What is Research & Development Tax Relief?

Research & Development (R&D) tax relief is a government incentive designed to encourage innovation in UK businesses. Companies can claim enhanced tax relief on qualifying R&D expenditure, either reducing their Corporation Tax bill or receiving a cash credit if they are loss-making. The scheme rewards businesses that invest in innovation and technical problem-solving.

Who Can Claim R&D Tax Relief?

R&D tax relief is available to companies that undertake qualifying research and development activities:

Innovative Companies

Companies developing new products, processes, or services

Problem Solvers

Businesses solving technical problems or overcoming scientific uncertainties

Improvement Projects

Companies improving existing products, processes, or services

Various Sectors

Manufacturing, technology, engineering, software, pharmaceuticals, and more

R&D Tax Relief Rates (2026)

Understanding the relief rates and benefits:

Enhanced Deduction 186%

Of qualifying R&D costs (86% above normal 100% deduction) for SMEs

Payable Credit 14.5%

Of surrenderable loss for loss-making companies (SME scheme)

RDEC Rate 20%

Credit rate for large companies or SMEs not eligible for SME scheme

SME Scheme

Available to companies with fewer than 500 staff and turnover under €100m or balance sheet under €86m
