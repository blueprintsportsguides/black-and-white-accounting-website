Construction Industry Scheme (CIS)

Tax deduction scheme for construction work payments. We handle contractor and subcontractor registration, monthly returns, and CIS compliance.

What is the Construction Industry Scheme (CIS)?

The Construction Industry Scheme (CIS) is a tax deduction scheme that applies to payments made by contractors to subcontractors for construction work. Contractors must deduct tax from payments to subcontractors and submit monthly returns to HMRC. The scheme ensures tax is collected at source from construction industry workers.

Who is Affected by CIS?

CIS applies to contractors and subcontractors in the construction industry:

Contractors

Businesses or individuals paying for construction work

Subcontractors

Businesses or individuals carrying out construction work

Construction Companies

Builders, developers, property companies, and construction firms

Tradespeople

Plumbers, electricians, carpenters, and other construction trades

CIS Deduction Rates & Deadlines (2026)

Understanding CIS deduction rates and filing requirements:

Standard Rate 20%

Deduction if subcontractor is registered with HMRC

Higher Rate 30%

Deduction if subcontractor is not registered

Gross Payment Status 0%

No deduction if subcontractor has gross payment status

Monthly Return Deadline

19th of each month following the tax month (e.g., 19 February for January)
