Value Added Tax (VAT)

Consumption tax on goods and services. We help with registration, scheme selection, returns, MTD compliance, and VAT planning to maximise efficiency.

What is Value Added Tax (VAT)?

Value Added Tax (VAT) is a consumption tax charged on most goods and services in the UK. Businesses with taxable turnover above £90,000 (2026 threshold) must register for VAT and charge VAT on their sales, while reclaiming VAT on their purchases. VAT-registered businesses must submit regular VAT returns and comply with Making Tax Digital (MTD) requirements.

Who Needs VAT Registration?

VAT registration is mandatory or optional depending on your turnover:

Mandatory Registration

Turnover above £90,000 in any 12-month period

Voluntary Registration

Businesses below threshold can register voluntarily

Importers/Exporters

Businesses importing or exporting goods may need to register

VAT Rates & Schemes (2026)

Understanding VAT rates and available schemes:

Standard Rate 20%

Most goods and services

Reduced Rate 5%

Domestic fuel, energy-saving materials, some mobility aids

Zero Rate 0%

Most food, books, children's clothing, public transport

VAT Schemes

Standard, Flat Rate, Cash Accounting, Annual Accounting, and Retail schemes available
