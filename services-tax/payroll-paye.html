Payroll & PAYE

PAYE (Pay As You Earn) system for collecting Income Tax and National Insurance from employees' salaries. Includes RTI submissions, payslips, and statutory payments.

What is Payroll & PAYE?

PAYE (Pay As You Earn) is the system HMRC uses to collect Income Tax and National Insurance contributions from employees' salaries. Employers must operate PAYE, submit Real Time Information (RTI) to HMRC each time they pay employees, and handle statutory payments like sick pay, maternity pay, and paternity pay.

Who Needs Payroll & PAYE?

PAYE applies to all employers with employees:

All Employers

Any business or organisation with employees

Limited Companies

Companies with directors on payroll

Sole Traders

Sole traders employing staff

Partnerships

Partnerships with employees

Charities

Charitable organisations with employees

Public Sector

Public sector organisations and government bodies

Tax & NI Rates (2026)

Current Income Tax and National Insurance rates:

Income Tax

20% (basic rate), 40% (higher rate), 45% (additional rate)

Employee National Insurance

12% on earnings between £12,570 and £50,270, 2% above

Employer National Insurance

13.8% on earnings above £9,100

Apprenticeship Levy

0.5% for employers with payroll over £3m
