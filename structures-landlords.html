Landlords

Specialist tax and accounting support for landlords. Whether you own one property or a portfolio, we help you navigate property tax, rental income reporting, and landlord-specific compliance.

What is Property Income Tax?

Property income tax applies to rental income from UK and overseas property. Landlords must report rental income and expenses through Self Assessment (for individuals) or Corporation Tax returns (for companies). Recent changes have reduced mortgage interest relief and introduced new reporting requirements. Understanding property tax rules is essential for maximising allowable expenses and minimising tax liability.

Who Needs Landlord Tax Support?

Understanding who needs this type of support helps ensure you're getting the right advice.

Individual Landlords

Individuals renting out residential or commercial property

Property Companies

Limited companies holding property investments

Portfolio Landlords

Landlords with multiple properties or high rental income

Accidental Landlords

Those renting out property due to relocation or inheritance

HMO Landlords

Landlords operating houses in multiple occupation

Furnished Holiday Lets

Landlords with furnished holiday lettings (special tax treatment)

Key Requirements (2026/27)

Important deadlines and requirements:

  • Rental Income Reporting Report all rental income and expenses in Self Assessment or Corporation Tax return
  • Mortgage Interest Mortgage interest relief restricted to basic rate (20%) for individuals
  • Property Allowance £1,000 property allowance available if gross rental income under £1,000
  • Capital Gains Tax CGT payable on property disposal (28% for higher rate taxpayers)
