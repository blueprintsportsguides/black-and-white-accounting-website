Partnerships

Specialist accounting support for traditional partnerships. We handle partnership accounts, profit allocation, and ensure each partner's tax obligations are met correctly.

What is a Partnership?

A partnership is a business structure where two or more people carry on a business together with a view to profit. Unlike limited companies, partnerships don't have separate legal personality - partners are personally liable for business debts. Partnerships must prepare accounts to calculate each partner's share of profit for their individual Self Assessment returns, and file a partnership tax return (SA800) with HMRC.

Who Operates as a Partnership?

Understanding who needs this type of support helps ensure you're getting the right advice.

Professional Partnerships

Solicitors, accountants, doctors, and other professionals

Trading Partnerships

Businesses with two or more owners sharing profits

Family Partnerships

Family members running a business together

General Partnerships

Any business with multiple owners operating as a partnership

Legal Requirements (2026)

Important deadlines and requirements:

  • Partnership Tax Return File partnership tax return (SA800) by 31 January following the tax year end
  • Partner Self Assessment Each partner must include their share of profit in their Self Assessment return
  • Record Keeping Keep business records for at least 5 years after the 31 January filing deadline
  • Profit Allocation Accounts must clearly show each partner's share of profit or loss
