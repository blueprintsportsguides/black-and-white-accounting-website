We are seeing an increasing number of HMRC enquiries where dividend payments are being reviewed, so we want to highlight an important compliance point for your limited company.

If you declare and pay dividends during your accounting period, the company is legally required to prepare and retain:

  • Board minutes (or a written director's resolution) approving the dividend; and
  • A dividend voucher for each shareholder at the time the dividend is paid.

These are not optional documents. They form part of the company's statutory records. If they are not in place at the time the dividend is paid, HMRC can challenge the treatment and potentially reclassify the payments as salary, which may result in additional income tax, National Insurance, interest and penalties.

We are also seeing more lenders and mortgage brokers request these as proof of earnings to support mortgage and loan applications.

Many directors understandably transfer funds without preparing the formal paperwork, but the documentation must support the dividend at the time it is declared.
